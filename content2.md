1. Digital painting (procreate)

Artist’s Statement / Creative Motivation
This series was born from my personal reflection on how beauty standards across different cultures and historical periods have often acted as a form of control over women. While clothing, makeup, and adornments were outwardly seen as signs of status or elegance, they also symbolized deeper social constraints — expectations of silence, obedience, and performance.
I was particularly interested in how beauty could become a burden, where the female body is shaped to meet the desires of a male-dominated society. Through digital painting, I wanted to reframe these historical images with a more critical eye — not to simply replicate tradition, but to question it. I chose figures from medieval Europe, Qing dynasty China, and Edo-period Japan because these eras represent highly stylized and codified forms of female appearance, where identity was often reduced to aesthetics.
My goal was not only to represent these women visually, but to express the emotional cost behind their appearances — the physical discomfort of tight corsets and bound feet, the psychological pressure of perfection, and the quiet sadness beneath painted faces. In doing so, I hope to challenge how we look at "beauty" in both the past and present.
